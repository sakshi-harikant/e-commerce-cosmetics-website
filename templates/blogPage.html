Morning Skincare Routine

Start your day with a gentle cleanser to remove overnight buildup. Follow with a vitamin C serum to brighten and protect your skin from environmental damage.

Apply a lightweight moisturizer with SPF 30 or higher to hydrate and shield your skin from UV rays. For oily skin, use a gel-based moisturizer. Dry skin types should opt for cream-based formulas with hyaluronic acid.

Wait 1 minute between applying serum and moisturizer to allow proper absorption. Don't forget your eye cream to reduce puffiness and dark circles.

Makeup That Lasts All Day

Begin with a primer suited to your skin type to create a smooth base. For long-lasting foundation, apply with a damp beauty sponge and set with translucent powder.

Use waterproof mascara and eyeliner to prevent smudging. For lips, line and fill them completely with lip liner before applying your lipstick. Blot with tissue and reapply for extended wear.

Spritz your face with setting spray after makeup application and again midday for refreshment. Cream products tend to last longer than powders.

Nighttime Skin Repair

Double cleanse in the evening - first with an oil-based cleanser to remove makeup and sunscreen, then with a water-based cleanser to clean the skin.

Apply a treatment serum with active ingredients like retinol or niacinamide to target specific concerns while you sleep. Follow with a richer night cream to lock in moisture.

Apply skincare products in order of thinnest to thickest consistency for best absorption. Use a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent sleep wrinkles.
